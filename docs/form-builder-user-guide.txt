=====================================================================
ZANBI FORM BUILDER — USER GUIDE
Design your own inspection report, your way.
=====================================================================

The Form Builder lets you design the printable report your clients
receive. You lay out the design once — your company header, the client
info block, the walkthrough sections — and every report you generate
after that follows your design automatically, no matter how big or
small the inspection was.

You design on your computer, in your web browser, where you have a big
screen and a mouse. Your phone is only used to get you in the door.


---------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OPENING THE FORM BUILDER
---------------------------------------------------------------------

1. In the Zanbi app, go to Settings and find REPORT DESIGNER.
   (Only the owner of your organization sees this section.)
2. Tap "Get editor link".
3. Tap "Share" to email the link to yourself, then open it on your
   computer — or tap "Open" to take a quick look on your phone.

That link is private to your company. Anyone who has it can edit your
report design, so treat it like a key:

  - The link works for 90 days, then you'll need a fresh one.
  - If you ever think someone else got hold of it, tap "Regenerate"
    in Settings. That creates a new link and instantly kills the old
    one everywhere.

The first time you open the Form Builder you won't see a blank page —
we start you off with a complete working report design. Feel free to
move things, restyle them, or delete them entirely. You can't break
anything; every report design can be rebuilt in minutes.


---------------------------------------------------------------------
2. FINDING YOUR WAY AROUND
---------------------------------------------------------------------

The screen has four areas:

  TOP BAR      — your template's name, Undo / Redo, zoom, the save
                 status, and the Publish button.
  LEFT PANEL   — everything you can add: Sections, Elements, Shapes,
                 and Data Fields. You DRAG items from here onto the
                 page.
  THE PAGE     — the white sheet in the middle. This is your report.
  RIGHT PANEL  — the details of whatever you have selected: position,
                 size, colors, fonts, and more. Click anything on the
                 page and this panel changes to match it.

The bar along the bottom of the screen has quick reminders of the
shortcuts that make editing faster.


---------------------------------------------------------------------
3. SECTIONS — THE BUILDING BLOCKS
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Your report is a stack of horizontal sections, from top to bottom.
There are two kinds, and the difference is the most important idea in
the whole Form Builder:

STATIC SECTIONS
  Appear exactly once in the report. Use these for things you only
  need one time: the report header, the client's name and address,
  a summary at the end.

REPEATING SECTIONS  (marked with a purple stripe)
  This is where the magic happens. A repeating section is a TEMPLATE
  that gets stamped out once for every area you inspected. Design it
  once — for example: section name at the top, a severity tag in the
  corner, notes below, photos at the bottom. Then, if your walkthrough
  covered Basement, Roof, and Foundation, the finished report
  automatically shows that same layout three times — once with the
  Basement's notes and photos, once with the Roof's, once with the
  Foundation's. Ten areas? Ten copies. You never touch the design.

Working with sections:
  - Drag "Static Section" or "Repeating Section" from the left panel
    onto the page. A purple line shows where it will land.
  - Hover over a section to reveal its toolbar: move up / move down,
    duplicate, and delete.
  - Drag a section's bottom edge to make it taller.
  - Click a section's name tag to select it; use the right panel to
    rename it or switch it between Static and Repeating.

Because repeating sections grow with each report, the number of pages
takes care of itself. The dashed "Page 2" lines on the design page are
just a rough guide.


---------------------------------------------------------------------
4. ELEMENTS — WHAT GOES INSIDE A SECTION
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Drag any of these from the left panel into a section:

TEXT
  A block of words you write yourself. Double-click it to start
  typing. While you're typing, a small toolbar appears with Bold,
  Italic, Underline, and a text color picker — select some words and
  style just those, exactly like a word processor.

FIELD LINE
  A line that gets filled in. Point it at one of your data fields
  (see the next chapter) and the real value prints there. Leave it
  unmapped and it prints as a blank line — perfect for anything you
  like to fill in by hand. Choose between three looks in the right
  panel: a simple underline, a box, or plain text.

DIVIDER
  A horizontal rule for visually separating content. Adjust its
  thickness and color in the right panel.

PHOTO GRID
  A grid of photo placeholders. Put one inside a Repeating Section
  and each area's photos fill it automatically in the finished
  report, with their captions underneath. Choose 2, 3, or 4 columns,
  the spacing, and whether captions show.

IMAGE / LOGO
  A picture that's part of your design — your company logo in the
  header, a certification badge, a signature image. Drop it on the
  page, then use "Upload image" in the right panel to pick a PNG or
  JPG straight from your computer. The picture always fits inside
  its box without stretching, and big files are scaled down
  automatically. Upload once — it prints on every report.

Moving and sizing things:
  - Click to select. Drag to move. Pull the corner handles to resize.
  - Pink lines appear when an element lines up with its neighbors —
    let go and it snaps perfectly into alignment.
  - Hold Alt while dragging to place something freely, with no
    snapping.
  - Arrow keys nudge the selected item one pixel (hold Shift for
    bigger steps).


---------------------------------------------------------------------
5. SHAPES — BACKGROUNDS AND ACCENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Rectangles, ellipses, and lines live BEHIND your text and fields,
always. Use them for colored header bars, soft background panels
behind a block of fields, or accent lines.

  - Drag a shape into a section, then style it on the right: fill
    color, border, rounded corners, and transparency.
  - Shapes never cover your text — anything you drop on top of a
    shape stays readable on top of it.
  - With a section selected, the right panel lists all of its
    background shapes so you can grab one even when it's behind
    other things.


---------------------------------------------------------------------
6. DATA FIELDS — WHERE YOUR REAL DATA SHOWS UP
---------------------------------------------------------------------

The pill-shaped items at the bottom of the left panel — like
"Customer Name" or "Address Line 1" — are live data fields. They're
the bridge between the app and your report: when a report is
generated, each one is replaced by the actual value from that
inspection.

Two ways to use them:

  1. DROP ONE ONTO THE PAGE
     It becomes its own field element you can place and style.

  2. DROP ONE INTO THE MIDDLE OF A SENTENCE
     Drag the pill directly into a Text block you're editing and it
     lands right where your cursor points, as a small purple chip.
     For example:
        "This report was prepared for {Customer Name} on
         {Report Date}."
     prints as:
        "This report was prepared for Sarah Mitchell on June 5,
         2026."

One rule to remember:
  Fields in the "Walkthrough Section" group — Section Name, Section
  Notes, Severity — change with every area of the home, so they only
  make sense inside a REPEATING section. If you drop one into a
  static section, the Form Builder outlines it in red and the right
  panel explains the problem. Move it into a repeating section and
  the warning disappears.


---------------------------------------------------------------------
7. SAVING AND PUBLISHING
---------------------------------------------------------------------

You never need to hit save. A moment after every change, the Form
Builder saves automatically — watch the save status in the top bar
("Saving…" then "All changes saved").

Your edits go to a private DRAFT. Nothing about your real reports
changes until you press PUBLISH in the top right. That's your "go
live" switch: publish when you're happy, and every report generated
from then on uses the new design. Until then, you can experiment
freely.

If you open the editor in two places at once and they step on each
other, the editor notices and asks you to reload rather than letting
one overwrite the other. Work from one tab at a time and you'll never
see that message.


---------------------------------------------------------------------
8. SHORTCUTS WORTH KNOWING
---------------------------------------------------------------------

  Ctrl + Z            Undo            (Cmd on Mac)
  Ctrl + Shift + Z    Redo
  Ctrl + D            Duplicate the selected item
  Delete              Remove the selected item
  Arrow keys          Nudge 1px       (with Shift: 10px)
  Alt while dragging  Place freely, no snapping
  Double-click text   Edit the words
  Escape              Stop editing / deselect


---------------------------------------------------------------------
9. IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG
---------------------------------------------------------------------

"This link isn't valid anymore"
  Your link expired (they last 90 days) or someone regenerated it.
  Open Zanbi on your phone → Settings → Form Builder → Get editor
  link, and use the fresh one.

"Save failed — retrying"
  Usually a brief internet hiccup. The editor keeps retrying as you
  work; once the connection is back, the status returns to "All
  changes saved". Avoid closing the tab while it shows unsaved
  changes.

"This template was changed somewhere else"
  The design was edited from another tab or computer since you opened
  this one. Click "Reload latest" to pick up the newest version, then
  continue.

A field prints blank in reports
  Check two things: the element is mapped to a data field (right
  panel → "Maps to"), and walkthrough fields live inside a repeating
  section — look for the red outline that marks a misplaced field.

Deleted something by accident
  Ctrl + Z brings it right back — the editor remembers your last
  60 steps.
